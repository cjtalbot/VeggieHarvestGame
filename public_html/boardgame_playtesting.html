Play-Testing Notes

Below are some of the notes that were taken during the playtesting activities for this game.

  • Thursday, September 22, 2011 In-class
    • Players asked about who gets which chips
    • Players asked which card deck was which
    • Players didn't know how to plant the plants - kept cards on the squares
    • Players forgot to add the chips after each round, but eventually added on their turn (liked that change)
    • Players didn't draw the correct number of cards, so didn't destroy plants
    • Wasn't clear where to place plants when their soil was full, so didn't plant at all
    • Not clear on what can kill with the destroy cards - were destroying entire plant as "1" plant instead of one chip
    • Noticed didn't really read/understand rules too well

  • Thursday, September 22, 2011 In-Lab
    • Players not clear that must play all cards in same turn, were saving cards
    • Players remembered to grow their plants
    • Some rules were missing from this version of the game
    • Good suggestion for new game name - Veggie Stackers

  • Saturday, September 24, 2011 At-Home
    • One player understood the scoring well, the other understood how to play cards well
    • Players forgot to grow their plants each turn
    • Was confusion over the first turn since no plants existed to grow
    • One player used only strategy to grow, never destroyed except when rolled a 2
    • Other player used the negative scoring to their advantage and only destroyed plants that counted positively for the opponent.
    • Players understood the planting for soils that were full
    • Players did not remember to grow their extra volcanic plant, or add chips per turn

  • Tuesday, September 27, 2011 Game Night
    • First Session 6:17pm-6:43pm
      • Used poker chip toss to determine first player
      • Wasn't clear on the difference between the same-soil squares
      • Not sure on what to do with the poker chips
      • Read rules while they played, not before
      • Played plants on same squares
      • Thought the destroy cards only destroyed own plants
      • When grew new plant for the volcanic soil, they grew another tomato on the volcanic soil
      • Thought had to destroy entire number on card
      • Not clear on what a "plant" was
      • Stopped drawing the destroy cards - saw it as a penalty
      • Near end, saw the losing points if on wrong soil
      • Unclear on whether need to plant your cards no matter what or discard - they discarded them
    • Second Session 6:52pm-7:15pm
      • Both players took about 3 -5 minutes to read the rules but didn't read them in entirety.
      • The players didn't understand how to start the game (who goes 1st).
      • The players had to reread the rules several times during the game to understand the basic game mechanics and gameplay.
      • The players didn't place the cards in the discard pile and copied one another instead of reading the rules.
      • Players didn't grow plants before continuing with their turn.
      • Players finally figured out the the usage of the white plant chips and where to discard the cards (face-up).
      • Players didn't place the white plant chips on the top of the stack of red/blue chips.
      • One player thought that the size of the game board squares was for the placement of the growth cards.
      • Even with the clarity of the rules of the die (0,1,2), both players still used and interpreted "1" incorrectly.
      • Players had to keep referring to the rules and didn't understand the concept of stacking = growing of plants.
      • At this point, players figured out how to add one chip to their stack at the beginning of each turn.
      • Players finally found out how to use white plant chips correctly.
      • Both players only plucked cards from the growth deck instead of incorporating strategy with destruction cards.
      • One player said the rules are a little vague and complex.
    • Third Session 7:44pm-7:50pm
      • One player liked the idea that the game is eco-conscious.
      • One player couldn't understand the rules at all.
      • The players didn't place the cards in the discard pile and copied one another instead of reading the rules.
      • Players didn't grow plants before continuing with their turn.
      • One player quit so the game ended.
    • Fourth Session 8:00pm-8:35pm
      • Didn't pick up on the growing the volcanic plant
      • Liked environmental pictures and concept
      • Unclear on how to get started
      • Didn't plant on the wrong soils
    • Fifth Session 8:48pm-9:06pm
      • Players didn't play the cards correctly and didn't add chips at the beginning of each turn.
      • Players didn't understand the concept of the destruction cards (that it's UP TO that amount on the card).
      • One player was trying to avoid rolling a two because they didn't understand the strategy.
      • One player said to reduce the number of pieces involved.
      • One player said make getting into the rules easier and show the boards at a couple of different stages on the rule sheet.
      • One player said make the scoring section more relevant (by possibly moving it to the top of the rule sheet) to know that strategy could be invoked by the way the game is scored.
    • Sixth Session 9:41pm-10:05pm
      • Explained the rules for expediency on this round
      • Played well & correctly
      • A few clarifications during the game to reinforce the rules for double planting and volcanic plant
      • Seemed to understand some of the strategy behind the game
